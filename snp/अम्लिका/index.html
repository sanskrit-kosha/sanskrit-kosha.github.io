amlikā

(1) TAMARINDUS INDICA LINN. (Ainslie 1, p. 425-428; Avk; Bpn, p.
581; Dgv, nr. 120; Dutt; DWH 1, p. 532-536; Dy, p. 270-272; Gul;
HK: amlīkā; Kav; KB 2, p. 887-890; MW; Na 1, nr. 178; Nadk. 1,
nr. 2434; PW; Roxb., p. 530; Sanyal; V 1, p. 145; Vśs);
(2) OXALIS CORNICULATA LINN. (Ainslie 2, p. 324-326; Chopra; MW;
Nadk. 1, nr. 1777) = O. MONADELPHA ROXB. EX WIGHT ET ARN. (Vśs,
= cāṅgerī) = O. PUSILLA SALISB. (PW, = kṣudrāmlikā = cāṅgerī);
(3) RUMEX DENTATUS LINN. (V 1, p. 63); [Page-526+ 35]
(4) MANGIFERA INDICA LINN. (Vśs);
(5) SPONDIAS MANGIFERA WILLD. (Vśs, = mācikā = āmrātaka);
(6) PHYLLANTHUS NIRURI LINN. (KB 3, p. 2225-2227);
(7) = palāśīlatā (PW; Vśs); acc. to KB 1, p. 788-789 and Nadk. 1,
nr. 397 palāśīlatā is BUTEA SUPERBA ROXB.;
(8) Kav (footnote p. 352): “Cakra says (on Ca. Sū. 27, 121) that it
is a little shrub growing in Kāmarūpa and its neighbourhood; Gaṅgā-
dhara believes that amlikā is otherwise called amlārdraka, a sour va-
riety of ginger; the plant is difficult to identify.”
